Instruction Sheet

Welcome to the GAME OF TRUST

This experiment aims to study behavioral patterns of trust behavior in virtual economic environments. Your valuable participation will contribute to advancing our understanding of behavioral patterns of human trust behavior in such settings.

If you have questions or need help, please raise your hand, and an experimenter will assist you. Please remain silent and courteous during the game, basing your decisions on your judgment. Kindly review and follow the instructions provided. We appreciate your cooperation.

Game Rationale:

The game is based on a simple question,

“DO YOU TRUST YOUR ANONYMOUS PARTNER?”

Participants will have the option to decide whether they want to trust a stranger or not with their received endowments. The points earned by participants in the game will be influenced not only by their judgments but also by the mutual trust and reciprocity displayed by their anonymous counterparts.

The participants can choose between maximizing personal winning versus maximizing overall group winnings. Remember the group that displays the maximum cooperation will be rewarded at the end of the game and the group with the least cooperation will be penalized.

Show-Up Fee:

Every player will minimum receive 200(Two Hundred) Taka, for their valuable participation upon completion of the experiment in addition to their game winnings.

Consent form and Log in:

Participants will sign a consent form for voluntary participation in the research project and receive a 6-letter token to log into the game.

Group and Roles:

Groups of two randomly selected participants will alternate between the roles of 'Sender'/'Trustor' and 'Receiver'/'Trustee'. Initially, one member assumes the 'Sender'/'Trustor' role while the other takes the 'Receiver'/'Trustee' role. Roles will reverse in subsequent rounds, ensuring each participant plays both roles. Participants will remain anonymous to their group mates throughout the experiment.

Rounds and Rankings:

1. Round Number: The game will have a total number of 04(Four) rounds.

2. Trust Evaluation Stage: The first two rounds, termed the 'Trust Evaluation Stage', will assess reciprocity between players in the group and rank their reputation based on observed data.

3. Ranking: After the first two and final four rounds, groups will be ranked based on aggregate trust ratings and published. Each participant can access their trust ratings and earnings, as well as those of their counterparts, to inform their decisions in subsequent rounds.

4. Reputation Signal Stage: In the 'Reputation Signal Stage', players make informed decisions based on their prior knowledge of trust levels within their group and their group's standing among all groups.

Endowment:

At the beginning of each round, the 'Sender'/'Trustor' in each group will receive a random number of endowments, which may vary from round to round and between groups. These endowments hold monetary value, so careful consideration is advised when making decisions.

Multiplication Factor:

The amount sent by the 'Trustor'/'Sender' will be multiplied by a fixed factor (known only to the 'Trustor') before reaching the 'Trustee'/'Receiver'. When the 'Receiver'/'Trustee' reciprocates, the multiplication factor will be 1.

Example: If a Sender decides to reciprocate 500 points from his given 1000 points to his/her counterpart, the receiver/ trustee will get,

500 x 2 =1000 points, when the multiplication factor is 2,

500 x 3 =1500 points, when the multiplication factor is 3 and so on

Trust Game Rules:

1. The 'Sender/Trustor' has the option to trust or not. If they choose not to trust, they input 0. If they choose to trust, they can input a percentage from 1 to 100. Example:

If total distrust: 0

If 40% trust: 40

If Total trust: 100

2. If the 'Sender/Trustor' does not trust their counterpart, the 'Receiver/Trustee' receives nothing. If the 'Sender/Trustor' trusts their counterpart, the 'Receiver/Trustee' receives the reciprocated amount times the multiplication factor. Example:

If the Sender trusts 30%, the Multiplication factor is 2 and the Endowment is 3000, the Receiver will be reciprocated = 0.3 x 2 x 3000 = 1800

If the Sender trusts 70%, the Multiplication factor is 3 and the Endowment is 2000, the Receiver will be reciprocated = 0.7 x 3 x 2000 = 4200

3. Now the 'Receiver/Trustee' must decide whether to trust back. They input 0 if they choose not to trust or a percentage from 1 to 100 if they choose to trust.

4. In the next round, the role will be reversed between the players in each group and thus the game will continue in all four rounds.

Reward and Penalty:

The team with the highest trust rating will have their game-winning amount doubled for each member as a reward. Conversely, the team with the lowest trust rating will face penalties. In the lowest-trust group, only the member with the lowest trust rating will receive a 75% deduction, resulting in them receiving only 25% of their game-winning amount.

Cash-out and Payment:

At the end of the game, each participant receives a participation fee of 200 takas. Game winnings, adjusted for rewards/penalties, will be converted into a specific percentage of taka and disbursed to participants. Participants submit their tokens, receive payments, and sign the payment sheet.

If any participant violates the rules of the game, he/she will be excluded from the experiment and will not be paid any fee. If any participant leaves before the experiment is concluded, he/ she will not be paid any fee.
